Chronic Disease Management

Dealing with chronic health issues may be difficult, even in the best of circumstances. Community First will assist you in navigating the problems of chronic diseases, such as diabetes, asthma, and chronic pain. 

FAQ

What is Chronic Disease Management?

Chronic disease management enables people with long-term health issues to live happy lives and be as healthy as possible. Several people suffer from one or more chronic diseases. It can have a significant influence on your quality of life, impairing your ability to work, conduct daily duties, and enjoy leisure time.

If you have a chronic health condition, the Community First team can help ypu create a customized care plan for you. Your plan covers all necessary routine tests and exams, progress evaluations, and other services that promote your general health. 

By delivering fluids and nutrients directly to your bloodstream, you can immediately experience benefits in your body. When taken orally, vitamins and fluids must transit through the digestive tract, reducing absorption.

Vitamins, minerals, medicines, and fluids administered intravenously begin working swiftly to restore and enhance your organs, energy, and immunity.

What does a chronic disease management plan contain?

Your chronic disease management plan is developed along with your Community First provider. When determining the necessary treatment for your disease, your provider considers all factors that could affect your health.

These may include your age and medical history, any family history of certain disorders, your work and home situations, and factors such as your food and whether you smoke or drink a lot of alcohol. Depending on your needs, your treatment plan may include medication, physical therapy, nutrition counseling, and other therapies.

For many people with chronic diseases, mental health issues can coexist with physical health issues. When managing a chronic disease, it is vital to take a holistic approach and provide emotional support.
